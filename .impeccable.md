## Design Context

### Users
Primarily parents of kids involved in productions — checking audition dates, performance schedules, buying tickets, and managing their child's participation. Secondary: general community members considering attending a show. Tertiary: adult performers auditioning or managing their own involvement. The key behavioral pattern is task-oriented: "When is the show? How do I get tickets? What does my kid need for auditions?" They are not browsing for entertainment — they want clear answers fast.

### Brand Personality
**Excellent, Accessible, Community**

This combination is intentionally humble. "Excellent" is about the experience of using the site and attending shows — not a claim to broadcast. "Accessible" means the door is genuinely open: no jargon, no barriers, no intimidation. "Community" means real people, local relationships, warmth without corporate distance.

The tone is: a trusted neighbor who happens to put on really good shows. Not a brand. Not an institution. Not a fundraising vehicle.

**Anti-patterns to avoid:**
- Award-winning, professional-quality, or any self-promotional language in UI copy
- Donation asks, support-the-arts guilt, or financial urgency anywhere in the interface
- Theatrical drama that sacrifices clarity (beautiful but confusing navigation, hero sections that bury the schedule)
- Corporate polish that feels distant or intimidating to a first-time community member

### Aesthetic Direction
The existing codebase establishes a dark navy + gold palette with Playfair Display headings, Bebas Neue labels, and Inter body text. This foundation is appropriate — theatrical without being overdone.

**Theme:** Dark (warm navy base). Keeps the theatrical specialness without feeling cold. Gold accents create warmth and hierarchy.

**Visual tone:** Warm theatrical restraint. Elegant but not self-congratulatory. The photography should do the emotional heavy lifting; the UI chrome should be quiet and purposeful. Think printed theatre program meets modern editorial — not a Broadway marketing campaign.

**Admin interface:** Utilitarian. Same color system for coherence, but leaning toward cleaner layouts, denser information, and less ornament. Parents managing forms and schedules need efficiency, not atmosphere.

### Design Principles
1. **Information first, atmosphere second.** A parent should find the show date within 3 seconds. Theatrical beauty must never create friction.
2. **Let photos carry the story.** The copy stays modest and factual. Images of real productions are the strongest statement of quality.
3. **The door is open.** Nothing should feel exclusive, expensive, or intimidating. Language and layout should signal that everyone belongs here.
4. **Restraint over proclamation.** Whitespace, clear hierarchy, and concise labels over decorated sections with marketing headlines.
5. **Admin is a different register.** The parent portal and admin tools share the brand but prioritize function — denser tables, less ornament, faster task completion.
